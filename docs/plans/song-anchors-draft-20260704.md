# M-SONG-ANCHORS: Song Anchors for Scale/Mode Teaching (DRAFT)

**UNVERIFIED-BY-OPERATOR** - candidate list only (agents draft, operator vetoes, per vision Q3). Veto/swap in any shorthand; survivors become the app's curated anchor data. Tags: CANONICAL (textbook-cited) / STRONG (widely agreed) / DISPUTED (contested - the dispute stated inline, never asserted as fact). Drafter ran 11 targeted web checks; three first-draft attributions were corrected by them.

## Major (Ionian)

| Song | Artist | Why it anchors | Tag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Let It Be | The Beatles | Plain I-V-vi-IV, melody entirely major scale - as vanilla-major as pop gets | STRONG |
| Brown Eyed Girl | Van Morrison | G major I-IV-V-vi bar-band staple, riff and melody diatonic | STRONG |
| Stand By Me | Ben E. King | The 50s I-vi-IV-V; bassline and vocal both major-scale | STRONG |
| Ho Hey | The Lumineers | Campfire/ukulele major-key stomp, easy sing-along | STRONG |
| Somewhere Over the Rainbow (Iz) | Israel Kamakawiwo'ole | THE ukulele-repertoire major anchor | STRONG |

## Natural minor (Aeolian)

| Song | Artist | Why it anchors | Tag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Losing My Religion | R.E.M. | Mandolin riff + full chord set diatonic to A natural minor, zero accidentals | CANONICAL |
| Nothing Else Matters | Metallica | Em-Am-Bm incl. the MINOR v (Bm) - the natural-minor tell (harmonic minor would major it) | STRONG |
| Zombie | The Cranberries | Em-C-G-D vamp = i-bVI-bIII-bVII, no raised 6th/7th | STRONG |
| House of the Rising Sun | The Animals | DISPUTED/mixed: Am-C cells are natural minor, but D major borrows dorian's raised 6 and the E major borrows harmonic minor's raised 7 - a great real-world "pure labels rarely survive a song" lesson | DISPUTED |

## Dorian

| Song | Artist | Why it anchors | Tag |
|---|---|---|---|
| So What | Miles Davis | THE textbook dorian example; D dorian A-sections, Eb dorian bridge | CANONICAL |
| Oye Como Va | Santana | Am7-D9 vamp - the D's F# is A dorian's defining raised 6 against the Am tonic | CANONICAL |
| Scarborough Fair | Traditional (S&G) | Probably the most famous dorian folk tune - m3, M6, m7 melody | CANONICAL |
| Eleanor Rigby | The Beatles | DISPUTED/mixed: Aeolian chords, one dorian melodic moment (the C# on "in the church") - documented mode mixture | DISPUTED |

## Mixolydian

| Song | Artist | Why it anchors | Tag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Louie Louie | The Kingsmen | A-D-Em: the recurring MINOR v (Em) instead of major V is the mixolydian tell | CANONICAL |
| Hey Jude (outro) | The Beatles | The na-na-na F-Eb-Bb = I-bVII-IV; one of pop's most famous bVII moments | CANONICAL |
| Fortunate Son | CCR | G-F-C verse = I-bVII-IV; the F is the flat-7 color on a straight-major riff | STRONG |
| Sweet Home Alabama | Lynyrd Skynyrd | DISPUTED: often called D mixolydian; the careful read is G major with the riff camped on V - genuinely argued, not settled | DISPUTED |

|---|---|---|---|

## Blues scale (minor pent + b5)

| Song | Artist | Why it anchors | Tag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pride and Joy | Stevie Ray Vaughan | Solo leans on the b5 blue note throughout - blues SCALE, not just pent | CANONICAL |
| The Thrill Is Gone | B.B. King | Bends explicitly target the b5; canonical vocabulary lesson | CANONICAL |
| Crossroads | Cream (R. Johnson) | Standard blues-scale solo vehicle over moving I-IV-V | STRONG |
| Smoke on the Water | Deep Purple | 4 notes of G minor pent PLUS the Db blue note - the pent-to-blues upgrade in one riff (parallel 4ths) | STRONG |
