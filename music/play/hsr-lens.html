The Cycle

Start with the home shape as I. HAMMER into a different shape that gives IV at the same hand position (no big jump). SLIDE +2 that same shape up two frets to get V (because IV-to-V is always a whole step in major-key diatonics). ROTATE up 1 fret into a new shape family to land on I again, one CAGED position higher. Repeat - the cycle keeps walking up the neck. Move between shapes in whatever order makes musical sense, but economy of movement is the constraint.

Shape families on uke (G C E A): treat G-C-E as if it were guitar D-G-B and let the A string be a color decision. The three shape families that drive the cycle: C-shape, F-shape, A-shape.

Mental model in one sentence

Ukulele G-C-E preserves guitar D-G-B interval geometry, so the same triad fragments transfer; HSR is a movement vocabulary (hammer / slide +2 / rotate +1) that walks I-IV-V up the neck through C, F, and A shape families instead of memorizing isolated chord charts. Open chords are entry points; movable inversions are the navigation system.

Three-string mode (mute A)

When A is muted, the C-shape I becomes [0,0,0,-1], F-shape IV becomes [2,0,1,-1], etc. Same fret motion, A doesn't sound. This is the guitar D-G-B bridge in pure form.
